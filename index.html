Femina’s Covers: 1959–2024

A Digital Humanities Analysis of Colour & Language

Project Abstract

This project analyzes over 220 cover pages of Femina (1959–2024) to trace how India’s leading women’s magazine visually and textually constructs the "new" Indian woman. By combining text mining of cover lines with digital color sampling, we reveal a shift from muted, earthy tones to high-saturation global aesthetics, paralleled by a linguistic transition from domesticity to "unstoppable" consumerist empowerment.

Methodology

1. Data Collection: Transcribed cover text and sampled 9–11 dominant hex codes per cover using ImageColorPicker from a corpus of 220+ issues.
2. Text Analysis: Utilized Voyant Tools for distant reading, identifying word frequencies and collocations across seven decades.
3. Visual Analysis: Aggregated hex codes to derive a 10-colour representative palette per decade, visualized through a custom HTML/CSS interface.

Decade-wise Colour Palettes

Textual Evolution

Analysis via Voyant Tools reveals a significant discursive shift. While "Beauty" and "Fashion" remain persistent, the 2010s and 2020s see a massive surge in the term "Unstoppable", signifying a move toward "popular feminism" and career-oriented self-fashioning.


  • 1950s/60s: Focus on "Flair", "Diwali", and "Charmers".
  • 1990s: Liberalization era introduces "Supermodel", "Designer", and "Substance".
  • 2020s: Peak frequency of "Unstoppable", "Power", and "Digital".
